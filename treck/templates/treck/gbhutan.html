Bhutan At Glance

Drug Yul, the Dragon kingdom, as Bhutan is known, is perhaps the last of the “Shangri-La”. The kingdom that has been hidden for centuries has preserved a wealth of sights. This small Himalayan kingdom has a population of 600,000 people. Bhutan follows lamias and it is still a way of life. Bhutan situates between china in the north and India in the south has preserved a unique charm to offer you once in your lifetime. It is a country of Nature, culture and Tradition. For Trekkers Bhutan is paradise in it real sense. Trekkers through the hill slopes of the country means treading through the rare Botanical plants, encountering colorful birds and rare animals like Takin and Blue sheep. Bhutan is also known for it’s colorful festivals with mask dances depicting its rich cultural heritage with deep-rooted religious meaning.

Restrected Areas

Not all areas or religious establishments are open to tourist. This is to ensure that monastic life can continue unhindered and environmental and safety concern. We will provide you with a full list of places and festivals that are open to tourist.

Transport

Toyota buses; Toyota cars and Toyota four wheelers Drive are all fitted with AC and heaters.

Meals

Continental Chinese, Indian and Bhutanese cuisines are available. Traditional Bhutanese food is very hot and spicy.

Time

Bhutan’s Time is six hour ahead of Greenwich Mean Time (GMT)

Local Currency

Bhutan’s unit of currency is the Ngultrum (NU), which equals to 100 Chetrum. A Ngultrum has the same value as an Indian Rupee.

FLight

Visitors can enter Bhutan by road through Indian territories at the border down of phunt sholing in the south of the country or by the Airline. Druk Air (KB, British Aerospace BAE 146) is the only national carrier servicing paro. There are three flights a week from Bangkok, two flights a week from New Delhi via Nepal and Calcutta.
